About Quaid-e-Azam

Muhammad Ali Jinnah (1876 ‚Äì 1948), known as Quaid-e-Azam ("Great Leader"), was the founding father of Pakistan. Born in Karachi, he studied law in London before becoming a prominent leader in the Indian National Congress and later the All-India Muslim League. His political vision, leadership, and determination led to the creation of Pakistan in 1947, where he served as the nation's first Governor-General until his death in 1948.
